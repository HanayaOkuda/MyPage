## *Physics behind Friction of Sheet-Structure Minerals*
Sheet-structure minerals are often found in natural faults and considered to be important for fault behaviors because of their mechanical weakeness. To investigate the controlling mechanisms of frictional characteristics of sheet-structure minerals, friction coefficients of sheet-structure minerals are theoretically derived focusing on the atomic-scale friction between two adjoining layers. We found that the friction coefficients of sheet-structure minerals could be explained by the variation of potential energy during sliding and yield strenghts of materials.

See also:
- H. Okuda, K. Kawai, & H. Sakuma (2019). First‐principles Investigation of Frictional Characteristics of Brucite: An Application to Its Macroscopic Frictional Characteristics. *Journal of Geophysical Reserach: Solid Earth*, **124(10)**, 10,423-10,443.
